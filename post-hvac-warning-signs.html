Is Your HVAC Telling You Something? 6 Sounds You Should Never Ignore

By Brayson · Rock Canyon Property Management · November 18, 2024 · 5 min read

At 7,000+ feet elevation, your heating system isn't just a comfort — it's life safety infrastructure. A furnace failure at midnight in January when temperatures drop to -10°F can mean frozen pipes, burst water lines, and tens of thousands of dollars in water damage before morning. And yet, most HVAC systems give plenty of warning before they fail. Here are six sounds your system makes when it's asking for help.

1. Banging or Clanking on Startup

A loud bang when your furnace kicks on is almost always one of two things: a dirty burner causing delayed ignition (the gas accumulates and then lights all at once — a mini-explosion), or a loose component in the blower assembly. Either issue can escalate quickly. Delayed ignition puts stress on the heat exchanger, and a cracked heat exchanger can leak carbon monoxide into your living space. Treat any banging as urgent.

2. High-Pitched Squealing or Screeching

A squealing sound from your air handler or furnace typically indicates a worn or slipping blower belt, or bearings on the motor beginning to fail. This is a mechanical warning that something is about to break. The good news: bearings and belts are inexpensive to replace when caught early. Ignored, a seized blower motor is a much larger repair — and in a vacation home with no one monitoring, you may not know until you arrive to a frozen property.

3. Rattling from Ductwork

Rattling from your ducts when the system runs can mean loose duct connections, debris inside the ducts (particularly common in vacation homes that sit idle), or even small animals that have found their way in. Loose duct connections reduce heating efficiency significantly — you may be paying to heat your attic or crawl space instead of your living areas. It can also mean that conditioned air is pulling in unconditioned or contaminated air from those same spaces.

4. Persistent Clicking That Won't Stop

A single click at startup and shutdown is normal — it's the relay switching. But continuous clicking that doesn't resolve means your igniter or flame sensor is failing. The system is trying to light but can't maintain a flame. This is both a performance issue and a safety concern. A failing igniter in a vacation home often means no heat at all the next time you or a tenant arrives. In Summit County winters, that's a property emergency.

5. Rumbling or Booming from the Furnace

Low rumbling or booming from inside the furnace cabinet — especially during operation rather than just at startup — can indicate burner problems or a dirty combustion chamber. Burners coated with dust and oxidation don't burn cleanly, leading to incomplete combustion, reduced efficiency, and carbon monoxide risk. Annual burner cleaning is not optional in mountain environments where homes sit idle and dust accumulates.

6. Hissing Near the Unit or Refrigerant Lines

If your home has a heat pump or air conditioning component, a hissing sound near the refrigerant lines or the outdoor unit indicates a refrigerant leak. Refrigerant leaks reduce heating and cooling capacity, can damage the compressor (a very expensive component), and — in older systems — may involve refrigerants that require EPA-certified handling. Any hissing near HVAC equipment should be inspected promptly.
